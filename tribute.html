About A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was a great scientist, teacher, and the 11th President of India. He played a major role in developing India's missile and nuclear programs. His life is a true inspiration for students and young minds.

Major Achievements

  • Developed India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • Key role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998)
  • 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna
  • Author of Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds & India 2020

Life Timeline

1931: Born in Rameswaram

1960: Joined DRDO

1969: Joined ISRO

1980: SLV-III successfully launched

1998: Pokhran Nuclear Test

2002: Became President of India

2015: Passed away while teaching students
